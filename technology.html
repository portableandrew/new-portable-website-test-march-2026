How we work

Co-sourced
delivery.

We don't build in isolation and hand over. We embed specialists into your existing teams, work alongside your people, and transfer knowledge through pairing, coaching and practice. When the engagement ends, your teams own the systems and the skills to evolve them.

This is not staff augmentation. It is deliberate capability building wrapped inside production delivery. You get working software and a stronger team at the same time.

01
Embed & pair
Our specialists sit inside your team. They pair with your engineers, share context, and build trust. Knowledge transfers through doing, not documentation.
02
Ship & uplift
Every sprint delivers working software and measurable capability growth. We track both velocity and team independence as first-class metrics.
03
Coach & scale
Delivery leads coach your emerging leaders. Engineering managers learn to build hiring pipelines, run retrospectives, and sustain culture without external dependency.
04
Exit & sustain
We plan the exit from day one. Runbooks, playbooks and capability roadmaps ensure your team can operate, maintain and evolve the systems independently.

Why Portable Technology

Consulting firms talk about transformation. We measure it.

01
Co-sourced, not outsourced
We embed inside your teams. Your engineers pair with ours. Knowledge transfers through practice, not slide decks. When we leave, you own the capability and the code.
02
DORA-tracked delivery
We benchmark against DORA metrics from day one. Deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate and mean time to recovery are measured, reported and improved every sprint.
03
All onshore. All senior.
No offshore handoffs. No juniors learning on your project. Every specialist we embed has deep production experience across cloud, security, delivery and modern engineering practice.
04
Built to exit
Every engagement is designed to end. We plan the transition from the first week, build runbooks and playbooks as we go, and track team independence as a core delivery metric. The goal is always self-sufficiency.
