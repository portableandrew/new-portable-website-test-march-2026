Objection Handling

What Steven might push back on

Click each to expand the response.
"We've tried AI tools before — our engineers aren't bought in" ▶
That's exactly why we don't start with tools. We start with the mobile app — something your engineers already own. Luke works alongside them on their actual codebase. They see what AI-first looks like on their own problems, not on demos. Buy-in follows results, not training.
"Why two people? Can't one person do this?" ▶
Luke is hands-on with your engineers every day — that's the value. Andrew isn't a layer on top of that; he's the person making the architecture calls and the harder strategic decisions so Luke can stay focused on delivery. They're complementary, not duplicative.
"3 months feels long — what are we actually getting?" ▶
A rebuilt mobile app, and an engineering team that can sustain AI-first practices without us. Most engagements leave a team dependent on the consultants. We're optimising for the opposite — you should need us less at the end of month three than you do at the start.
"We have engineers — why can't they just do this?" ▶
Your data analyst already proved they can — they built full production systems independently. The gap is that this hasn't become a team-level practice. What's missing isn't talent; it's someone who's done it before working alongside them to show it's possible at that scale.
"We're mid-transition on other things — is the timing right?" ▶
The mobile app rebuild is already on the roadmap — it needs to happen regardless. We're anchoring to something that was already a priority. This isn't an additional initiative; it's doing the thing you were going to do anyway, with the right approach embedded from the start.
"Have you done this exact thing before?" ▶
We're not claiming to have a fifty-client AI transformation playbook. What we have is technical depth to make real decisions, and a track record of figuring it out collaboratively alongside clients — the same way we worked with Morris Blackburn on Eleven Labs. That honest framing is deliberate.
